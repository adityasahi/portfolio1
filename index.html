About Me

Hello there, my name is Aditya Sahi.

I am a graduate student at Ohio University pursuing a Master’s in Electrical Engineering, building on a Bachelor’s degree in the same field. My academic journey has equipped me with a strong foundation in signal processing, machine learning, embedded systems, and software development. These complementary skills allow me to seamlessly innovate at the intersection of hardware and software, enabling solutions for complex technical challenges.
In my professional experience, I have contributed to impactful projects across IT, R&D, telecommunications industries. As a Graduate Research Assistant, I developed a sustainable, low-power Wireless Mesh Network (WMN) using the WI-SUN protocol and optimized signal processing for IoT-based weather sensors. My experience as a Research Assistant included conducting experiments with software-defined radios and developing strategies to mitigate voltage leakage in GPUs. Additionally, my role as a Graduate Teaching Assistant refined my ability to communicate complex concepts effectively while mentoring students.

Outside of academia, I actively participate in organizations such as IEEE, Game Developers Association, and Bobcats Esports, where I hone my technical and teamwork skills. My diverse technical proficiencies include coding in Python, C/C++, MATLAB, and x86 Assembly, alongside expertise in edge computing, machine learning, and protocol optimization. Recognized for my academic excellence and leadership, I am committed to leveraging my interdisciplinary background and passion for innovation to make meaningful contributions in both academic and industrial landscapes.
